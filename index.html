This involves identifying the key Defra business strategic goals and operational challenges and analysing which emerging digital technologies will best help achieve those objectives. Defra Group Business Trends

The Defra group Technology Innovation team continuously monitors emerging technology trends and Defra group business challenges and opportunities.

Welcome to the Defra DDTS Emerging Technology Radar. In a year when Defra group continues to face multiple significant challenges along with all Government departments, technology remains crucial for creating and enabling potential solutions. This report highlights the potential influence of emerging technologies on the challenges and opportunities faced by the Defra group.

Environmental Sustainability

Defra is the Green Lead for UK Government and has a key role to play in setting standards and lead by example for environmental best practice as shown through the commitments in the 25 year Environment Plan and Net Zero targets. Defra group played a key role at the 27 climate change summit in Egypt. This reflects Defra’s responsibility on behalf of the UK government for environmental sustainability, and as the lead for sustainable IT across government

The environmental sustainability challenges are impacting the entire Defra group and extend across government and industry sectors. Within the Defra group, DDTS leads the government’s efforts on sustainable Information Technology (IT), establishing best practices throughout the UK government and the digital technology industry. These include defining objectives in the Defra Sustainable Tech Strategy, forming the successful Defra e-Sustainability Alliance (DeSA) and recently launched Defra group Sustainability Strategy. Consequently, this year’s radar has been produced in collaboration with Defra group sustainability leads.

Digitial and Data Transformation

Defra group continues to have a key role to play in delivering the government’s 2022 to 2025 Roadmap for Digital and Data, Transforming for a Digital Future. The roadmap sets out the ambitions to transform digital public services, deliver world-class digital technology and systems, and attract and retain the best in digital talent. This must be achieved whilst delivering best value for money to the taxpayer, by transforming ways of working to enable the Civil Service to work smarter, faster and deliver on our ambitions for widespread digital transformation.

The COVID-19 pandemic has served as a powerful reminder of the unparalleled role that data plays in shaping government decision-making. The urgency to rapidly collect, analyse, and exchange data from diverse government sources to respond to the pandemic has emphasised the crucial significance of data in informing government responses. In response to this challenge, the National Data Strategy has taken a proactive approach to prioritise investment in this area, while also creating a roadmap to overcome the hurdles that prevent us from using data effectively. By doing so, the strategy aims to enable effective data-driven decision-making across government. For Defra group, effective data gathering, sharing, and analysis remain essential for enabling data-driven policy-making and operational interventions.

Security

The threat of cyber-attacks to Defra group is constantly increasing. If these attacks are successful, they can cause significant disruptions to essential services, result in the theft of sensitive information, or facilitate the spread of false information. To address these challenges, the UK Government has developed a roadmap for building a cyber resilient Public Sector that includes acquiring the necessary cyber skills and implementing secure systems and services. This roadmap complements the broader Government Cyber Security Strategy, which emphasises the importance of cyber resilience for the country’s security and economic wellbeing. AI and Quantum technologies have been identified as both a threat and opportunity for security..

New Ways of Working

Defra group, like many Public Sector bodies face increasing demands for flexible working arrangements. The desire for the equality of opportunity for everyone in the UK through levelling up, and the de-centralising initiative hoping to empower people across the UK regardless of location reinforce the necessity for technology to help smooth this transition. Emerging technology allows for remote collaboration, video conferencing, and instant communication.

Younger employees, in particular, expect to have access to these tools and are often frustrated by the outdated technology that is still prevalent in many public sector organisations. This shift towards more flexible working arrangements has created new challenges in attracting and retaining talent. To overcome these challenges, Defra group must invest in new technology, provide training to ensure that employees are proficient in its use, and create a culture that values innovation and flexibility. By doing so, they can attract and retin the best talent, improve productivity, and better serve the needs of citizens.

DEVELOPING THE RADAR

We update the Logistics Trend Radar every two years to reflect how we at DHL view the key social, business, and technology trends within the logistics industry.

Our unique and integrated customer-centric innovation approach empowers us to harness the most relevant perspectives for logistics transformation directly from our valued customers and colleagues engaged in daily operations. We complement these insights using classic research methodologies, and incorporate the work of multiple experts, including key opinion leaders from influential think tanks, renowned consultancy firms, and top-tier academia from around the world.

  • Megatrends
  • Macro Forces
  • Innovators

Major movements and patterns – namely globalization, e-commerce, digitalization, and sustainability – have gradually transformed the logistics industry over the past decades and will continue to do so in the future. We inspect how these megatrends further shape the needs of societies and industries and the supply chains that serve them.

As drivers of change extending beyond whole industries, macro forces influence the acceleration and importance of trends and their placement on our Trend Radar. We evaluate the growth and decline of macro forces over the last two years, as well as how they compound or conflict with one another. They are numerous, but some examples are societal values, demographics, climate change, the Covid-19 pandemic, urbanization, consumer behavior, geopolitical events, and labor shortages, among many others.

Researchers, inventors, developers, visionaries, and providers of technologies play a crucial role in the advancements that revolutionize the logistics industry. By collaborating with such innovators early on, we here at DHL derive invaluable insights into sophisticated technological breakthroughs and new possibilities on the horizon.

INNOVATION PHILOSOPHY

It is our core belief that delivering meaningful innovation can only be achieved by staying close to customers, close to technology, and close to operations:

Close to Customers

We cultivate honest conversations and engagement with our customers about business strategies, future needs, and pain points in order to validate ideas, develop use cases, and leverage technologies and expertise. We achieve success through strong customer buy-in and close collaboration.

Close to Technology

We connect the logistics and the digital worlds, tracking and engaging visionary developers and providers of all kinds and staying at the forefront of emerging technology and supply chain innovation. This enables us to identify and co-create new solutions beneficial to our customers.

Close to Operations

We leverage our own global DHL logistics operations to improve logistics efficiency, quality, and safety. The significant time we spend on site with facility managers and workers to visualize and implement solutions benefits both our customer and colleagues.

DRIVING INNOVATION

Our end-to-end innovation project approach starts with customer engagements, technology scouting, and research, which helps us understand social and business trends, as well as technological breakthroughs.

This forms the basis of our Logistics Trends Radar and additional thought leadership trend reports. With a finger on the pulse of the relevant trends, we run proof of concept projects in real-world supply chain settings with our customers and within the DHL network of operations to fully understand the benefits and challenges of applications. Successful projects open up opportunities for productization and scaling, so that these solutions are available internally across DHL operations and commercially to customers.

THE DHL INNOVATION CENTER NETWORK

With our four DHL Innovation Centers located in Germany, Singapore, the US, and the UAE, we spearhead the future of logistics and drive customer-centric innovation around the world. This global network enables us to consider regional socio-economic differences and actively participate in local innovation ecosystems.

Every year, we welcome tens of thousands of customers, partners, and other innovative thinkers and visionaries to engage with DHL experts and each other. Through these creative hubs, we facilitate and lead workshops, innovation center tours, events, and joint innovation projects to better understand customer needs and identify actions to solve key supply chain challenges. We also take a focused, user-centric, and systematic thought leadership approach to discovering impending developments, emerging best practices, potential industry applications, and the implications of these trends on logistics. Leveraging customer insights and research findings, we test, create, customize, commercialize, and scale innovative solutions for the benefit of our customers and our colleagues.
